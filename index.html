Sho-Me Power Core Values Project

Clarifying the values that guide how we work, serve members, and show up for each other.

Sho-Me Power is formalizing a set of Core Values that are clear, credible, and usable in everyday decisions and behavior across the organization. This work is part of the broader Culture of Excellence effort and builds on the Leadership Retreat held in May 2026.

Project Purpose & Team Role

Why this project exists

  • Capture the best of Sho-Me’s current culture.
  • Address areas where people experience confusion, silos, or inconsistency.
  • Provide a common language to guide decisions, behavior, and accountability across teams.

Role of the Core Values Definition Team

  • Turn existing inputs (like the Leadership Retreat work and other feedback) into a focused set of Core Values that fit Sho-Me.
  • Draft clear, plain-language definitions and everyday “what it looks like” behaviors for each value.
  • Provide input on how Core Values are introduced, reinforced, and maintained over time so they stay alive in daily work, not just at rollout.

What a Core Value Should Do Here

At Sho-Me Power, a Core Value should:

  • Set reasonable and clear expectations for how we want people to show up at work.
  • Help explain why people would want to work here and stay here.
  • Reinforce why team members can take pride in their work and in Sho-Me as an organization.

Core Values should feel real and believable. They should help people make decisions, navigate tradeoffs, and understand “how we do things here,” not just sit on a poster.

What We Want to Keep, Change, and Add

During the May Executive Retreat, leaders named what they want to keep, kill, modify, and add in Sho-Me’s culture. These lists are not final Core Values, but they are important raw material for this team’s work.

Keep

Protect and reinforce

  • Safety-focused
  • Career-minded tenure and longevity
  • Pride in the work
  • Member-focused
  • Flexibility
  • Collaboration
  • Accountability
  • Community
  • Having fun
  • Grit
  • Attracting and keeping the best talent
  • Autonomy
  • Partner-like relationships
  • Recognition
  • Growing trust
  • Efficiency
  • Excellence

Kill

Reduce or eliminate

  • Silos
  • Lack of trust
  • Over-focus on a “past ideal” instead of where we’re going
  • Rumor mill
  • Inconsistent accountability and “pockets” of different standards
  • Mob mentality
  • Unhealthy competitiveness
  • Company/union divide
  • Perceptions of poor communication
  • Burnout

Modify

Tweak and improve

  • Safety (how it’s understood and practiced)
  • Cross-functional collaboration
  • Recognition
  • Continuous improvement
  • Accountability
  • The right amount of employee involvement
  • Appropriate transparency
  • Cross-divisional relationships
  • Sense of community

Add

Build more of this

  • “One team” mindset
  • Discipline
  • Pride in the company
  • Strong member focus
  • Servant leadership
  • Strong sense of fit
  • Mutual respect

These lists are inputs, not final Core Values. The Core Values Definition Team will use them to spot patterns, name what matters most, and draft values that feel both real and aspirational for Sho-Me.

Project Milestones & Timeline

The Executive Team has set a clear path from defining Core Values to rolling them out across Sho-Me and maintaining them over time.

Spring–Fall 2026: Definition Phase

Core Values Definition Team meets regularly to review inputs, identify themes, draft values, and build clear definitions and behavioral examples.

By October 31, 2026: Values Named & Defined

A final set of Core Values, definitions, and “what it looks like” behaviors are agreed upon and ready for rollout planning.

Q1 2027: Organization-Wide Rollout

Core Values are introduced at an all-employee meeting and through multiple channels like meetings, communications, performance tools, and recognition.

Ongoing: Maintenance & Culture Champions

A longer-term Culture Club / Maintenance Team and leadership champions help keep the values visible and connected to real decisions, recognition, and accountability.
